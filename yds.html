YDS: The Yosemite Decimal System

YDS is a system of grading rock climbs developed in the US in the 1950s.

 

Class 1
  • walking on a trail
Class 2
  • walking off trail
  • very occasional use of hands
Class 3
  • scrambling on rock
  • occasional to frequent use of hands
  • generally no rope is used
Class 4 
  • climbing on steep terrain
  • requires use of hands and basic climbing skills
  • most parties will use a rope
Class 5
  • technical climbing
  • requires moves and techniques that a novice would not possess
  • most parties will use a rope, have fail-safe belays, and place protection between belays
  • an open-ended scale of increasing difficulty: 5.0, 5.1, 5.2, etc.
